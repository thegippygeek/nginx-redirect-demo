## Phase Goal

**As a** presenter, **I want to** show the 301 redirect approach on its own port right next to the proxied port, **so that** the audience sees the URL bar stay put in one case and visibly change in the other — the conceptual crux the whole cutover rests on.

<objective>
Deliver the contrast that gives the demo its meaning. A dedicated nginx listener on 9093 returns a 301 to the backend's real address; port 9092 keeps proxying transparently. Side by side, the audience sees the difference between "someone else answered on my behalf" and "you were sent somewhere else."

This plan also writes the presenter-facing `README.md` — the one-time `/etc/hosts` prerequisite, the port allocation and its narration, the command surface, and the 301-caching caveat that would otherwise silently break a second demo run.

Purpose: HTTP-03 and HTTP-04 are the conceptual payload of Phase 1. Without the contrast, the Phase 2 cutover is a party trick; with it, the audience understands why the proxy approach is the one that lets a migration happen without the client noticing.
Output: a 9093 listener in `proxy/nginx.conf`, a `redirect` section in `scripts/smoke.sh`, a `contrast` Makefile target, `README.md`, and a human verification checkpoint covering the browser proof and the ENV-03 inspection.
</objective>

Write the target literally rather than deriving the host from `$host`. Two reasons, both load-bearing.
