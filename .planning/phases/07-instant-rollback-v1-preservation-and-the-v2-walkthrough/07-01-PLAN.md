<objective>
Make "the old proxy is never touched" a verifiable fact and keep v1 runnable. The full v2
topology already flips both protocols and rolls back via one switch reload (`flip.sh old|new`) —
this plan adds ASSERTIONS over that unchanged runtime: a destructive `section_rollback` proving a
cutover-then-rollback returns BOTH protocols to OLD with no container teardown and with the two
static-proxy configs byte-identical (and not even reloaded) across the cycle, and a non-destructive
`section_preserve` proving the `v1.0` tag still holds a self-contained single-proxy demo. Both are
wired into `make test`.

**Phase user story (MVP):** **As a** presenter, **I want to** roll back to OLD instantly by flipping
the switch back — and prove by checksum that the two static proxies were never touched — **so that**
"the old proxy is never touched" is a verifiable claim and v1 stays runnable from its preserved form.

Purpose: this is the Slice A demoable milestone — rollback is instant and the checksum is a proof,
not a claim; v1 is preserved. Output: two new smoke sections and their wiring, no runtime change.

assumption-delta / api-coverage / schema-gate: N/A — POSIX-sh assertions over an unchanged runtime;
no external API, no ORM/schema, no new pluralization. No package installs (no npm/pip/cargo surface).
</objective>
