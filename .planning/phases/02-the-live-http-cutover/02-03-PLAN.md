<binding_contract>
`02-UI-SPEC.md` is an APPROVED design contract, not a suggestion. Every token, dimension, hex value
and string in it is binding. Where this plan restates a value it is for the executor's convenience;
where they ever disagree, UI-SPEC wins. Read it in full before writing a line of markup.

Three of its rules are load-bearing enough to restate here:

- **The root scale hook divides a length by a plain NUMBER.** Dividing by a length is not permitted
  inside `calc()`; that form is invalid, the whole declaration is dropped, the root font size
  silently falls back to the 16px UA default, and the entire rem scale collapses to its 1920
  reference values. On a 1920 projector it still looks correct, which is what makes the bug
  dangerous. UI-SPEC gives the exact one-line form — copy it verbatim.
- **Panel separation is carried by the 2px border, not by the fill.** The secondary fill sits at
  only 1.6:1 against the ground and is the first thing heavy projector washout destroys. An
  implementation that relies on the fill alone looks correct on a laptop and dissolves into one
  flat field on the projector.
- **Motion is rationed to exactly three uses**: the one-shot convergence sequence, the PENDING
  pulse, and the footer heartbeat dot. Nothing else animates, ever — no spinners, no skeletons, no
  counter roll-ups, no row slide-ins, no easing on the poll refresh.
</binding_contract>
