    Assert the typography limits by extracting every distinct `font-size` declaration from
    `status/index.html` and requiring exactly four, and every distinct `font-weight` declaration and
    requiring exactly two. The two-weight limit is not stylistic: the system font stack resolves to
    a different family per OS and intermediate weights either synthesise or snap unpredictably
    across those families, so 400 and 700 are the only cuts guaranteed real everywhere. A page that
    looks right on the presenter's Mac must look the same on a borrowed Windows laptop.

    Assert the colour-as-fill rule: each of the two backend accent hexes appears in the file only on
    a `background` or `background-color` declaration, and never on a `color` or `border-color`
    declaration. As fills behind white text they are compliant; as text on the dark ground they
    would fall to roughly 2.7:1 and fail. Assert that every panel selector carries a 2px hairline
    border, because the secondary fill sits at only 1.6:1 against the ground and is the first thing
    heavy projector washout destroys — an implementation relying on the fill alone looks correct on
    a laptop and dissolves into one flat field on the projector.

    Assert the root-scale form: the length-divided-by-a-plain-number expression is present and the
    invalid divide-by-a-length form is absent. The invalid form is dropped silently, the root size
    falls back to the browser default, and the whole rem scale collapses to its reference values —
    which still looks correct at 1920 wide, and that is what makes the bug dangerous.

    Assert zero non-localhost absolute URLs in the file, covering the offline test. Assert the
    markup-parsing assignment property does not appear at all, so the rendering discipline that
    keeps an attacker-controlled request path inert on the projector cannot be undone by a later
